# Stuxnet (2010) :

    1. Target Attack : It is mainly created to exploit the industrial control systems (ICS) of Iranian nuclear facilities.

    2. Physical Damages : What is happening is that, the attacker make the centrifuges causing them to spin abnormal speed and make them in to a destruction.

    3. Sophistication : It's a complex malware which bypass the security and also believed to be sponsor by state which it is an expert of the field.

    4. Atrribution : It is beilieved to be made by the US, although the goal is to destroy the iran's nuclear program without war conflict.

    @ Impacts :

    Stuxnet proved cyberattacks could cause physical damage.
    It increased awareness of cyber threats to critical infrastructure.
    Raised ethical questions about using technology for harm.

# Loveletter (2000) :

    1. Spread: Sent as email attachments with subject lines like "ILOVEYOU."
    2. Infection: Executed when attachments were opened, infecting computers.
    3. Corruption: Overwrote files, rendering them unusable.
    4. Self-Preservation: Disabled antivirus and modified system settings to avoid removal.

    @ Impacts :

    Widespread: Infected millions of computers globally.
    Data Loss: Corrupted and deleted files, causing significant data loss.
    Economic Damage: Cost billions due to downtime and lost productivity.
    Awareness: Increased focus on cybersecurity and prevention measures.

# Equifax Breach (2017) :

    1. Vulnerable software: Equifax was using outdated software with known security flaws.

    2. Lack of proper security measures: The company failed to implement sufficient security measures to protect sensitive customer data.

    3. Human error: Mistakes made by Equifax employees may have contributed to the breach.

    @ Impacts :

    Gather information: Learn about the target's vulnerabilities.
    Exploit weaknesses: Find and use security holes.
    Gain access: Break into the system.
    Steal data: Copy sensitive information.
    Maintain access: Stay in control for future attacks.

# SolarWinds Hack :

    1. Target: SolarWinds, a software company that provides network management tools.

    2. Method: Hackers inserted malicious code into SolarWinds' software updates, allowing them to gain backdoor access to any organization using the compromised software.

    3. Impact: Numerous government agencies, including the Department of Homeland Security, the Treasury Department, and the State Department, were affected. Private companies, such as Microsoft and Intel, were also compromised.

    4. Attacker: The attack is widely attributed to a Russian state-backed hacking group known as APT29, also known as Cozy Bear.

    5. Consequences: The SolarWinds hack exposed sensitive government data, disrupted critical services, and eroded trust in cybersecurity infrastructure. It also led to increased scrutiny of supply chain security and the potential for geopolitical tensions.
