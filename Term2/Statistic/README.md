## **1. Types of Data for Statistical Analysis**

### **Raw Data**
- **Definition:** Data collected in its original form.
- **How to Find:** Gather directly from the source.
- **Examples:**
  - Non-numeric (e.g., gender, education level)
  - Numeric with true zeros (e.g., test scores, age)

### **Group Data**
- **Definition:** Raw data organized into categories or intervals.
- **How to Find:** Arrange data in a frequency distribution table.

---

## **2. Types of Statistics**

### **Frequency Distribution**
- **Definition:** Organizes data by counting the frequency in each category.
- **How to Find:** Tally counts for each group.

### **Descriptive Statistics**
- **Definition:** Summarizes data using numbers and graphs.
- **How to Find:** Compute measures such as mean, median, mode, and range.

---

## **3. Measures of Central Tendency**

### **Mean (Arithmetic Mean)**
- **Definition:** The average value.
- **Formula:**  
  \[
  \text{Mean} = \frac{\sum X}{N}
  \]
- **Components:**  
  - \(\sum X\): Sum of all values  
  - \(N\): Total number of values

### **Median**
- **Definition:** The middle value when data is ordered.
- **How to Find:** Sort the data; choose the central value (or average of two middle values if even).

### **Mode**
- **Definition:** The most frequently occurring value.
- **How to Find:** Identify the value with the highest frequency.

---

## **4. Measures of Variation**

### **Range**
- **Definition:** The difference between the highest and lowest values.
- **Formula:**  
  \[
  \text{Range} = \text{Max Value} - \text{Min Value}
  \]

### **Quartile Deviation**
- **Definition:** Half the difference between the first (Q1) and third (Q3) quartiles.
- **Formula:**  
  \[
  \text{Quartile Deviation} = \frac{Q3 - Q1}{2}
  \]

### **Standard Deviation**
- **Definition:** How much values deviate from the mean.
- **Formula:**  
  \[
  \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\sum (X - \text{Mean})^2}{N}}
  \]

### **Percentage Value**
- **Definition:** The proportion of a category relative to the total.
- **Formula:**  
  \[
  \text{Percentage} = \frac{X \times 100}{N}
  \]
- **Components:**  
  - \(X\): Count for the category  
  - \(N\): Total count

---

## **5. Inferential Statistics**

### **Chi-Square Test**
- **Definition:** Compares observed vs. expected frequencies for categorical data.
- **Formula:**  
  \[
  \chi^2 = \sum \frac{(O - E)^2}{E}
  \]
- **Components:**  
  - \(O\): Observed frequency  
  - \(E\): Expected frequency

### **T-Test**
- **Definition:** Compares means between two groups.
- **How to Find:** Use formulas for independent or paired samples.

### **Z-Test**
- **Definition:** Compares means or proportions with large samples or known variance.
- **Formula:**  
  \[
  Z = \frac{\text{Sample Mean} - \text{Population Mean}}{\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{N}}}
  \]

### **ANOVA (Analysis of Variance)**
- **Definition:** Compares means across three or more groups.
- **How to Find:** Calculate the F-statistic comparing between-group and within-group variances.

### **Regression Analysis**
- **Definition:** Analyzes relationships between a dependent variable and one or more independent variables.
- **How to Find:** Use least squares or other fitting methods to create a predictive model.

### **Correlation Analysis**
- **Simple Correlation:**  
  - **Definition:** Measures strength and direction between two variables (e.g., Pearson's r).
- **Multiple Correlation:**  
  - **Definition:** Evaluates relationship between one dependent variable and several independent variables.

### **Inference Statistics**
- **Definition:** Draws conclusions about a population from a sample.
- **How to Find:** Apply statistical tests and calculate confidence intervals.

## **6. Research Objective, Hypothesis, and Proposal Writing**

### **Research Objectives**
- Define the study’s purpose and goals.
- Direct data collection and analysis.
- Ensure research focus and relevance.

### **Variable Identification**
- **Independent Variable:** Factor manipulated.
- **Dependent Variable:** Factor measured.
- **Control Variable:** Factor held constant to reduce bias.

### **Research Framework**
- Describes relationships between variables.
- Guides research design and analysis.

### **Research Hypothesis**
- **Null Hypothesis (H₀):** Assumes no effect or difference.
- **Alternative Hypothesis (H₁):** Assumes a significant effect or difference.
- **Testing:** Use statistical methods to validate.

### **Population & Sample Selection**
- **Population:** Entire group under study.
- **Sample:** A representative subset.
- **Sampling Methods:** Random, stratified, or systematic.

### **Sample Size Calculation**
- **Formula:**  
  \[
  n = \frac{N}{1 + N(e^2)}
  \]
- **Components:**  
  - \(n\): Sample size  
  - \(N\): Population size  
  - \(e\): Margin of error

### **Research Methodology**
- **Qualitative:** Descriptive methods (interviews, observations).
- **Quantitative:** Statistical analysis (surveys, experiments).

### **Data Collection Methods**
- Surveys, interviews, observations, experiments.

### **Statistical Analysis Methods**
- **Descriptive:** Mean, median, mode, standard deviation.
- **Inferential:** T-test, chi-square, ANOVA, regression.
- **Correlation:** Simple and multiple correlation.

### **Research Proposal Structure**
- **Chapter 1:** Introduction (background, problem, objectives, hypotheses)
- **Chapter 2:** Literature Review (related theories and studies)
- **Chapter 3:** Methodology (design, sample, data collection)

### **Ethical Considerations**
- Informed consent, confidentiality, and objectivity.

## **7. Hypothesis Testing Summary**

### **Research Hypothesis**
- Predicts relationships between variables.
- Examples: Salary satisfaction differences, customer satisfaction variations.

### **Statistical Hypothesis**
- **Null Hypothesis (H₀):** No difference or effect.
- **Alternative Hypothesis (H₁):** A significant difference or effect.

#### **Key Statistical Terms**
- **Mean:** \( \bar{X} \) vs. \( \mu \)
- **Standard Deviation:** \( S \) vs. \( \sigma \)
- **Variance:** \( S^2 \) vs. \( \sigma^2 \)
- **Correlation:** \( R \) vs. \( \rho \)
- **Proportion:** \( P \) vs. \( \pi \)

### **Hypothesis Testing Process**
1. Define **H₀** and **H₁**.
2. Choose significance level (α, typically 0.05).
3. Select appropriate test (t-test, chi-square, etc.).
4. Compute test statistic and p-value.
5. Decision Rule:  
   - If **p ≥ α**: Accept **H₀**.  
   - If **p < α**: Reject **H₀**.

### **Types of Hypothesis Tests**
- **One-Sample T-Test:** Compares sample mean to a population mean.
- **Paired-Sample T-Test:** Compares two related groups (e.g., before and after).
- **Independent-Sample T-Test:** Compares means of two independent groups.

### **One-Tailed vs. Two-Tailed Tests**
- **One-Tailed Test:** Tests for an effect in one direction.
- **Two-Tailed Test:** Tests for any difference regardless of direction.
