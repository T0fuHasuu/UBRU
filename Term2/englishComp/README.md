# Tense

## **1. Present Simple**  
### **Form:**  
**Subject + base verb** (add **s/es** for third person singular)  
### **Usage:**  
Expresses general truths, habits, and repeated actions.  
### **Example:**  
"She codes every day."

---

## **2. Present Continuous (Progressive)**  
### **Form:**  
**Subject + am/is/are + *verb*‑ing**  
### **Usage:**  
Describes actions happening at the moment or temporary situations.  
### **Example:**  
"He is debugging the program."

---

## **3. Present Perfect**  
### **Form:**  
**Subject + have/has + past participle**  
### **Usage:**  
Indicates actions completed at an unspecified time before now, often with present relevance.  
### **Example:**  
"They have completed the project."

---

## **4. Present Perfect Continuous**  
### **Form:**  
**Subject + have/has been + *verb*‑ing**  
### **Usage:**  
Emphasizes the duration of an action that began in the past and continues into the present.  
### **Example:**  
"I have been learning Python for months."

---

## **5. Past Simple**  
### **Form:**  
**Subject + past form of the verb**  
### **Usage:**  
Refers to completed actions or events in the past.  
### **Example:**  
"She compiled the code yesterday."

---

## **6. Past Continuous (Progressive)**  
### **Form:**  
**Subject + was/were + *verb*‑ing**  
### **Usage:**  
Describes actions that were ongoing at a specific moment in the past.  
### **Example:**  
"They were coding when the power went out."

---

## **7. Past Perfect**  
### **Form:**  
**Subject + had + past participle**  
### **Usage:**  
Expresses an action that was completed before another action in the past.  
### **Example:**  
"He had fixed the bug before the release."

---

## **8. Past Perfect Continuous**  
### **Form:**  
**Subject + had been + *verb*‑ing**  
### **Usage:**  
Highlights the duration of a past action that was ongoing until another action occurred.  
### **Example:**  
"We had been testing the system for hours when the error occurred."

---

## **9. Future Simple**  
### **Form:**  
**Subject + will (or shall) + base verb**  
### **Usage:**  
Used for predictions, promises, or spontaneous decisions.  
### **Example:**  
"I will run the program tomorrow."

---

## **10. Future Continuous (Progressive)**  
### **Form:**  
**Subject + will be + *verb*‑ing**  
### **Usage:**  
Describes actions that will be in progress at a specific future time.  
### **Example:**  
"At 10 AM, she will be debugging the system."
